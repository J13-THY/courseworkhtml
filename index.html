Amazon Forest Wildlife

The Amazon Rainforest is one of the most biodiverse regions on Earth. It is home to thousands of species, including jaguars, macaws, poison dart frogs, sloths, and countless insects and plant life.

Often called the "lungs of the Earth," the Amazon plays a vital role in regulating the global climate and supporting ecological balance. Its rich ecosystem continues to inspire scientific research and conservation efforts worldwide.
